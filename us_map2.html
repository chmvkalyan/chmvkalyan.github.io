About the COVID-19 Risk Levels

Each countyâ€™s COVID-19 risk is primarily based on the number of cases reported per capita during the past 7 days. Additional precautions are suggested if more than 10 percent of tests had a positive result over the past 7 days of available data. This can mean that the county is not testing enough, and that the number of cases may be undercounted.

Although risk levels are assigned based on expert guidance and careful analysis, it is possible that the risk level in a specific county may be over- or underestimated because of a lack of reliable data.

A county is at

  • an extremely high risk for unvaccinated people if it reported an average daily rate of more than 45 cases per 100,000 people.
  • an very high risk for unvaccinated people if it reported an average daily rate of 16 to 45 cases per 100,000 people.
  • an high risk for unvaccinated people if it reported an average daily rate of 6 to 15 cases per 100,000 people.
  • an moderate risk for unvaccinated people if it reported an average daily rate of 3 to 5 cases per 100,000 people.
  • an low risk for unvaccinated people if it reported an average daily rate of less than 3 cases per 100,000 people.
