Vaccinations Per Country Over Time

The below map depicts the geographic distribution of vaccinations across the World. The color gradient changes from lighter pale green to darker pale green ranging from low to high "Vaccinations (per Hunderd)". Slider faciliates the option to choose a time frame.

More than 4.36 billion vaccines doses have been administered worldwide, equal to 57 doses for every 100 people. There is already a stark gap between vaccination programs in different countries, as this map shows.

A vaccinated person refers to someone who has received at least one dose of a vaccine, and a fully vaccinated person has received all required doses of a vaccine. For the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine, a person who is “fully vaccinated” has received two doses.

While vaccine doses remain relatively scarce globally, most countries have focused their early vaccination efforts on priority groups like the clinically vulnerable; people in their 60s, 70s and older; and front-line workers, like doctors and nurses.
